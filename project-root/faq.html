Frequently Asked Questions About Generative AI

1. What is Generative AI?

Generative AI refers to AI models that create new content, such as text, images, audio, or code, based on the patterns they learned during training.

2. How does it work?

It is trained on large datasets and learns patterns, allowing it to generate new content from user prompts.

3. What are examples of GenAI tools?

  • ChatGPT
  • DALL¬∑E
  • MidJourney
  • GitHub Copilot / Cody
  • Google Gemini

4. What can GenAI do (strengths)?

  • Generate text, code, and images quickly
  • Assist with brainstorming and creativity
  • Create summaries and explanations
  • Help with learning and study guides

5. What are its limitations or risks?

  • May provide incorrect or misleading information
  • Can be biased based on training data
  • Cannot replace human judgment or expertise
  • May encourage plagiarism if misused

6. Can I rely on GenAI for school work?

GenAI can help generate ideas and drafts, but always verify and rewrite outputs in your own words. Use it responsibly.

7. Is Generative AI free?

Some tools offer free versions with limits, while advanced features or higher usage may require subscriptions.

8. Can GenAI learn from my input?

Most models do not remember personal input across sessions, but they can adapt to prompts within the same conversation.

9. How can I cite AI outputs?

Always mention the AI tool, model version, and date when using AI-generated content in assignments or research.
